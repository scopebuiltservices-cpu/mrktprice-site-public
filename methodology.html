Methodology & Trust

MrktPrice is a market research and education terminal. This page states plainly how the numbers are produced, how confident you should be in each kind of claim, and — just as important — what the research does not prove. It is not investment advice.

Point-in-time discipline

Anything fed into the no-lookahead forecast ledger and information-coefficient estimates is gated so a value is only used once it was actually available at the decision time (filing lags applied). Current-vintage TTM/consensus fundamentals shown on the board are labeled as such and are not point-in-time — they are a present-day snapshot for context, not a backtest input.

Forecasts & calibration — the honest version

  • The projection cone is a calibrated reference range, not a point forecast. Its bands are widened by split-conformal calibration on matured (already-resolved) forecast residuals, with purge/embargo to avoid leakage.
  • The coverage guarantee we claim is marginal at the horizon level — historically, the band contained the outcome about as often as its target. Where sample permits we also report regime-conditioned coverage, but the headline guarantee is marginal, not a per-regime promise.
  • Distribution scores (CRPS, PIT) describe the Gaussian base predictive; the interval score and coverage describe the published conformal band. These are labeled distinctly so the two objects are never conflated.
  • Any ranked "edge" is deflated for multiple testing (deflated Sharpe) and, where the trial dispersion can't be measured, is flagged provisional. Backtested edge is in-sample evidence, not a promise of future edge.

How strong is each claim?

Every number on the terminal falls into one of these tiers. We try to label loudly and never let a strong word stand in for weak math.

What this does not prove

  • Forecasts are statistical, not advice. Calibrated intervals state how often outcomes have landed inside the band historically; they do not promise the next move.
  • Pooled skill is upward-biased by survivorship: the universe is current constituents; delisted names are absent, so historical hit-rates overstate a live, point-in-time strategy.
  • Macro/factor betas are associational, not causal — they describe co-movement, not a mechanism, and can break in a regime change.
  • Cross-language (Python/JavaScript) parity proves the two implementations agree; it does not prove the shared model is correct.
  • Numbers can be stale or degraded. Check the freshness banner and per-source timestamps before trusting a row.

Update cadence

The market map rebuilds on a nightly schedule plus an intraday 3pm-ET refresh, and self-heals: if the published data is stale or was produced by an older engine, the next run forces a fresh rebuild. Health probes run every few hours independently of the full build.
